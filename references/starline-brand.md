## Cohort Model

Explain the method simply:

- A cohort business makes marketing, product, and commercial decisions based on separate customer cohorts and their contribution to future revenue.
- Marketing mostly affects customers with no previous interaction: new customers.
- The marketing task is growth of the latest cohort.
- The business grows when revenue from the latest cohort is higher than the revenue churn of accumulated cohorts.

## Problem Narrative

Use this contrast:

- CPA can be optimized while the business does not grow.
- Attribution can make advertising look effective while GMV barely changes.
- Retargeting and old cohorts can create a false sense of performance.
- Cohort analysis links marketing to future e-commerce revenue.

## Process

Use three stages:

1. Audit.
2. Pilot.
3. Scaling.

Recommended expansion:

- Audit current advertising, cohorts, sales channels, GMV, CPA, DRR, and new-customer structure.
- Pilot growth hypotheses for the latest cohort on a controlled channel set.
- Scale working channels and connect analytics and forecasted economics.
